Middle School Outreach

A student who participated in MS Outreach said, “I’ve really enjoyed working with middle school students. It makes me happy to see how interested and engaged they are in the topics, and to see the schools incorporating STEM topics early in their classes now” During their visits at middle school, they did activities revolved around pseudo code and identifying similarities between their pseudo code and an example of code in C++, as well as teaching binary and creating bracelets with the 1s and 0s patterns to match their initials.

NIMS after school STEM program

The NIMS project was started in the spring of 2018 by a second year Ph.D. Industrial Engineering student. This spring the NIMS project was a seven-week project where students created simple robots with Arduinos. The curriculum combined and introduction to programming and circuitry skills. The Ph.D. student said, “I was very proud of the work the student did throughout the program. I felt like we hit a stride as a group and they were able to accomplish and learn a lot within those 7 weeks.”

High School Outreach

Similar to the MS Outreach, a group of students dedicated their time to the High School Outreach. While visiting the high schools, this group engaged students in pseudocode activities, and providing unbiased information about college and degrees and opportunities in STEM. A student who participated in HS Outreach said, “Having the opportunity to visit high schools and mentor students was a very rewarding experience. I was never afforded such an opportunity during my years in high school. I’m thankful to be able to give back to the Tallahassee community in this capacity, and potentially inspire these kids to continue to pursue an education after high school.”
